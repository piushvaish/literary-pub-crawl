Your Literary Journey Through Dublin's Pubs

  • Bleeding Horse: Mentioned in James Joyce's "Ulysses" and Flann O'Brien's works.
  • D2: A modern hub in Dublin's cultural quarter, popular among contemporary writers.
  • Neary's: Favored by Brendan Behan and Flann O'Brien, with a preserved Victorian interior.
  • McDaid's: Former morgue frequented by Patrick Kavanagh and featured in Joyce's "Grace".
  • Davy Byrnes: Famous setting in Joyce's "Ulysses" for Leopold Bloom's gorgonzola sandwich.
  • The Duke: Named after Wellington, loved by Oliver St. John Gogarty and James Stephens.
  • Toner's: W.B. Yeats' only pub drink, also visited by Patrick Kavanagh and Bram Stoker.
  • Waterloo: A historic finale with authentic Victorian ambiance.
