Your Literary Journey

  • Bleeding Horse: This pub, dating back to the 17th century, was mentioned in James Joyce's "Ulysses" and Flann O'Brien's "At Swim-Two-Birds."
  • D2: While not as old as some others, D2 is located in the heart of Dublin's cultural quarter and has become a popular spot for modern writers and artists.
  • Neary's: A favorite haunt of writers like Brendan Behan and Flann O'Brien, Neary's Victorian interior remains largely unchanged since the 19th century.
  • McDaid's: Once a morgue, this pub was a regular spot for Patrick Kavanagh and Brendan Behan. It's mentioned in James Joyce's short story "Grace."
  • Davy Byrnes: Famously featured in Joyce's "Ulysses," where Leopold Bloom stops for a gorgonzola sandwich and glass of burgundy.
  • The Duke: Named after the Duke of Wellington, this pub was a favorite of writers like Oliver St. John Gogarty and James Stephens.
  • Toner's: W.B. Yeats is said to have had his only pub drink here. It was also frequented by Patrick Kavanagh and Bram Stoker.
  • Waterloo: While not as famous for its literary connections, this historic pub provides a fitting end to the crawl with its authentic Victorian atmosphere.
